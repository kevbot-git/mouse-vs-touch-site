“Which out of mouse or touch input methods provide higher efficiency in pointing to objects that vary in size and position?”

Introduction

UI design has come a long way from early computers such as the Xerox PARC to today's elegant Macintosh interfaces. Over time we have optimized user interfaces to maximize visual appeal and functional usability, thanks to findings by the likes of Paul Fitts and Jakob Nielsen. This website is an interactive report based on a Human-Computer Interaction study carried out by AUT students Farley Schaefer and Kevin Menhinick. The study aims to answer the above research question; hopefully for the purpose of serving as a reference for developers or UI designers when considering pointing aspects of interaction with their UIs.

Purpose

The purpose of this study is to look at the relationship between a target’s size and screen position, and each input method’s efficiency in pointing to the target (in terms of time taken to point to and click); and to find out how the connection differs for each. We will have to design and carry out an abstract experiment that will produce results appropriate to be generalized; thus allowing them to potentially be used for a sort of benchmark for UI design as a whole.
